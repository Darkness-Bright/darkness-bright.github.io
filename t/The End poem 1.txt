The End poem: part 1

The End poem was written over 20 years ago now, and it has been read by so many people, and I am confident that it has got at least 300 million views already, probably making it the single most read poem ever.

To be fair, it's not that people bought a book full of poems and found The End poem to be a very interesting poem. Instead, the reason why so many people have read it is  because the poem is shown along with the credits of Minecraft. When you've beaten the game, the background would turn into the texture of a dirt block, and you must watch the poem scroll across the screen for around 10 minutes... The poem had no name, but it is always referred to as The End poem. After that the credits would roll. Finally, the player would be spawned back in the game.

And yet, as the players beat the game, they expected just a credits roll, but they got a poem instead. Not just a regular one, but a poem that has the potential to unlock a higher spirituality (I had no better phrase to describe this). The poem was too enigmatic for almost any person, so... they either forgot about the poem literally one minute later or they thought the game was trying to convince them to "get a life".

Some attempts at decoding this poem have been made, and some of them got as deep as deciphering that the poem tells people that life is actually a long dream that you could wake up from...?


That was a lot of context, but it is needed for this to be described properly.

Anyway, starting now, I will extract all useful information I can from The End poem. Put them in bullet points, and then hopefully make a whole reasoning network for the analysis of the poem.

############################################
Part 1: Information from outside of the poem

- The player has worked hard and beat the game.
- The player went into the portal under the dragon for the first time, therefore activating disappearing into nothing and activating the poem and the credits.

######################
Part 2: The two beings

- There are two beings talking to one another.
- They are apparently distinct from one another, as depicted by word color: blue and green.
- The green one seems to be more emotional or romantic.

- They have had many names: father sun, mother moon, animal spirits, ghosts, gods, demons, angels, aliens, leptons, quarks...
- They never change and pretty much have existed forever.
- They have a strong power of guidance.

#################################
Part 3: What the two beings think

- They are mildly aware of the fact the player could read their thoughts but they do not really care about that.
- They want the player to "wake up", which could mean:
  1. Wake up from the 'dream' of reading lines on a screen back into the game.
  2. Wake up from the game into real life, hence "get a life".
  3. Wake up from real life into... a higher level.
