Aliens. or as we call them, have been described in very many science fiction books. In this article I summarize the different images people tend to think of aliens and what they purpose they could possibly have.

First of all, the evolutionary fantasies group. Ever since people accepted the theory of evolution was made, many people agreed that life is extremely diverse and is able to change dramatically over a long period of time. Many studies prove that. As a result of this, some have fantasized aliens to be very demon-like, if I may use the phrase. Such authors imagine aliens to have tentacles, unusual amount of eyes or other sensory organs, and altogether creepy. Supernaturalness among aliens is an improbable phenomenon, but arguably they have a chance to appear because of their efficiency and longevity. 

On the other hand, many people would imagine aliens to be more human-like because, umm, they believe that the human body is great for doing a wide variety of things. Many aliens are described in this form for readers to better interpret their thoughts and emotions as they normally do.

It is an unavoidable fact that aliens will be different to people in many different ways.

And then, there's the reported cases of aliens 'kidnapping' people, which are true. Now, more and more people have seen the big-brained, curvy-eyed grey aliens. They are definitely watching over earth with everything they have now. (Including the moon...) 

As for what they want to do... Well, nothing not for their advantage. They could have spread the seeds of life, or earliest humans, on earth to watch them grow, and when they become civilized enough, make them join. Aliens have been hiding secretively for a very long time now, and it is not clear why. They, surely must have been very interested in humans, either because they genetically engineered the first humans or because they wanted to find something about humans that they think is mysterious.
